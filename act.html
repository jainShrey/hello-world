Guest Lecture2- Mr Nitish Kumar Luthra

Guest of honor: Mr. Nitish Kumar Luthra, General Manager, Haldor Topsoe India Pvt. Ltd. 

On the 17th of October, the Department of Chemical Engineering and the students under it were enlightened about the demand for advancements in the field of hydroprocessing and catlysts. This was carefully taught by Mr. Nitish Kumar Luthra, General Manager at Process Refinery, Development and Engineering Production at Haldor Topsoe. The event began with by following the lighting of the ceremonial lamp and the Sararwati Vandana. The Chancellor Dr. S.J. Chopra and the Head of the Department Dr. I.D. Mall personally welcomed Mr. N.K. Luthra to the university.

He was clearly impressed by the welcome given to him and appreciated the university and its method of bringing forth budding engineers. Without further ado, he delved into his topic which was “Hydroprocessing: Its needs and Challenges”. For nearly an hour and a half, he captivated the students as they made to understand working of the hydroprocessing profession and the importance of improving the hydroprocessing techniques currently employed. He also explained the history of Haldor Topsoe and how the company manages to satisfy the various orders down to the slightest details for its large no. of customers. He also explained various other topics which included the importance of hydrogen in hydroprocessing, the difference between hydroprocessing and hydro-desulfurization and the various factors that affect the reaction kinetics of the reactions taking place in a reactor. It was quite the interactive session as many relevant questions were raised by students as well as well as from the faculty.

The event winded up with a summary to recall and jot down the important points discussed during the session. As a form of admiration and veneration, the Chancellor along with the rest of the dignitaries present gifted Mr. N.K. Luthra and his family a small token of respect in the form of a gift. Photographs were clicked and the event concluded with all the dignitaries present leaving for the lunch. The event managed to promote the motive of “teaching beyond the classrooms” and was added as another feather to the cap of the Chapter.


Attended By: Dr. S.J Chopra, Dr. I.D Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta, Dr. P. Vijay and other faculty members of the Chemical Engineering and Petroleum and Earth Sciences Department and students.

Guest Lecture1- Mr. Kartik Suryanarayan

Guest of honor: Mr. Kartik Suryanarayan Director (Engineering), UOP India Pvt. Ltd., Gurgaon

UPES witnessed the presence of an eminent personality from one of the world’s major technology providers, Honeywell UOP, Mr. Kartik Narayananan, Director (Engineering) at the headquarters of UOP LLC., in Gurgaon.

The event commenced with the Saraswati Vandana and the lighting of the ceremonial lamp by Mr. Kartik and some of the most celebrated faculty of the Chemical Department which included Dr. S.J. Chopra, Dr. I.D. Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta and Dr. P. Vijay. The occasion also had the highpoint of unveiling the first edition of IIChE UPES Student Chapter’s magazine “The Chemical Lifestyle”. Dr. S.J. Chopra welcomed Mr. Kartik to UPES and gave a great introduction about UOP LLC. to the spectators. Dr. I.D. Mall, the HOD of the Chemical Department then took the stage and introduced the guest of honor to the audience. Mr. Kartik took the stage and appreciated Dr. I.D. Mall for preparing a program where he could share his personal views on the crude oil prices, technology drivers and the skill set gap in the Indian workforce. He also appreciated the university about how it develops the students for an industry-centric career. The lecture covered a general area of how the oil market stands and the reasons for its fluctuations. It lasted for an hour and a half as Mr. Kartik carefully explained the world crude situation and how India stands a good chance as an emerging giant under the current conditions. He also talked about how the world and more importantly, India needs more innovative and creative engineers to solve the world’s problems. He also claimed that as engineers, we need to find ways to meet the energy requirements and to meet the ever-growing demands of the public with the resources at hand growing lesser day by day. The attendees of the lecture conducted by Mr. Kartik Suryanarayanan walked away in the end with a clearer picture and a better understanding of the oil economics and the geopolitical reasons behind the fluctuations of the oil prices.

The event turned out to be a grand achievement for the IIChE UPES Student Chapter and the Department of Chemical Engineering for it accomplished the objective of teaching the students about the current world scenario and the role of engineering in the advancement of the world.


Attended By: Dr. S.J Chopra, Dr. I.D Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta, Dr. P. Vijay and other faculty members of the Chemical Engineering and Petroleum and Earth Sciences Department
