Guest lectures(2015-16)


Year :   2015-16   2014-15

Guest Lecture 6 - Mr. Somaan

Guest of honor: Mr. Somaan. 
It has been a great honour for UPES, Dehradun and IIChE UPES Students Chapter to have been blessed with the esteemed presence of Mr. Somaan on 21st of April, 2016. Mr. Somaan shared his gallons of experience and unfathomable knowledge with the students. With seconds turning into minutes and minutes to hours, the words of wisdom encapsulated the entire hall into the strings of harmony and enlightenment. The topic for the occasion was “refinery operations” which was flawlessly presented among the audience. The session focused on the Industrial application of academic knowledge. Students were exceptionally impressed with his presentation skills. The topic selected for discussion was both apt and interesting. The final session of the day was the highlight of the eve. An interaction between aspiring engineers and giants of the industry is always fruitful and productive. With students putting up their ideas and being fed with solutions from the mainstream industry itself is a treat to watch and be a part of. The overall session was extremely useful and inculcated a sense of enthusiasm among the students in regard of the industrial world. Such sessions should be organized more often.
Attended By: Dr. S.J Chopra, Dr. I.D Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta, Dr. P. Vijay and other faculty members of the Chemical Engineering and Petroleum and Earth Sciences Department and students.

Guest Lecture 5 - Dr. A.B. Pandit

Guest of honor: Dr. A.B. Pandit, FNA, FASc, FNASc, FNAE from ICT-UDCT, Mumbai. 
On March 31, 2016, IIChE UPES Student Chapter had the extraordinary opportunity to organize a distinguished guest lecture by yet another erudite from the Chemical Engineering guild.Dr. A.B. Pandit, FNA, FASc, FNASc, FNAE from ICT-UDCT, Mumbai is both a gifted lecturer and a leading researcher. Dr. Pandit commenced the session by designating the audience of being a part of the “Chemical Engineering fraternity” and encouraging the budding minds to “give back to the society” with our knowledge and skills. He then focused on how even a simple piece of research worked upon with the aim of bettering the society can be as valuable as a high-end work. After giving such an igniting pep-talk, he then introduced the audience to the topic of his session, “Chemical Engineering Forays into Rural India” where he discussed the installation of a new pump in rural village in which the process of cavitation aided the removal of microbes from the water, thus purifying it. He also discussed an innovative design of “House Chula” and the resourceful application of solid fuel in rural villages .
Attended By: Dr. S.J Chopra, Dr. I.D Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta, Dr. P. Vijay and other faculty members of the Chemical Engineering and Petroleum and Earth Sciences Department and students.

Guest Lecture 4 - Dr. Suddhasatwa Basu

Guest of honor: Dr. Suddhasatwa Basu, Distinguished professor, IIT - Delhi. 
Walk with the dreamers, the believers, the planners, the doers, the successful people with their heads in the clouds and their feet on the ground. Let their spirit ignite a fire within you to leave this world better than when you found it for it is their experience that makes you learn the newfound ways of life.
In the light of this thought, IIChE UPES Student Chapter, in association with the Department of Chemical Engineering. organized the fourth Distinguished Guest Lecture The event was graced by Dr. Suddhasatwa Basu, Distinguished professor at IIT - Delhi.
In an hour long session, Dr. Basu engaged in a discussion on the photochemical pathways of CO2 reduction to Chemical Fuel and shared his treasured knowledge on the subject. In order to make the session interactive, a round of thought provoking question and answer round was also arranged. The session provided both the students and the coveted faculty members, with a new perspective on the discussed subject. The event concluded with the guest of honor being presented with a memento as a token of veneration and gratitude.
Attended By: Dr. S.J Chopra, Dr. I.D Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta, Dr. P. Vijay and other faculty members of the Chemical Engineering and Petroleum and Earth Sciences Department and students.

Guest Lecture 3 - Mr. Praveen Saxena

Guest of honor: Mr. Praveen Saxena, Director and CEO at Blast Carboblocks Pvt. Ltd. 

There are moments upon which hang the destiny of years. It is only for the moment that the favorable opportunity is presented. One such momentous opportunity, for both aspiring chemical engineers and veterans, was the third Distinguished Guest Lecture orchestrated by the IIChE UPES Student Chapter in association with the Department of Chemical Engineering. The event was graced by Mr. Praveen Saxena, Director and CEO at Blast Carboblocks Pvt. Ltd.
Erudite like Dr. I.D Mall, Dr. S.K Gupta and Dr. P.Vijay further embellished the occasion with their coveted presence. The event commenced with the lighting of lamp followed by the Saraswati Vandana. The acme of the event was the launch of IIChE UPES Student Chapter’s website, which was inaugurated at the hands of Mr. Praveen Saxena. The website was yet another milestone achieved by the Chapter in its incessant journey of success.
In an hour long session, Mr. Saxena, focused on the technical aspects of the oration, delivering a laudable lecture on “Modern Carbon Products and Processing” wherein he discussed in gravity, how graphite could be used as an “Energy efficient material” and the how his company Black Carboblocks Pvt. Ltd. aimed at achieving this target. Also, Mr. Saxena shared his galvanizing words of wisdom motivating the young audience to pledge to work hard and evolve as a community.
The event ended with Mr. Saxena bestowing a heat exchanger to the University as a gift and a token of good-will with the intention of benefitting young minds. Furthermore, he offered summer internship opportunities to interested students in future.
The event concluded at a high note with Mr. I.D Mall presenting the guest of honor with a memento as an insignia of respect and gratitude. Taken as a whole, the event was a huge success with students being enlightened about yet another aspect of the ever-dynamic Chemical Industry


Attended By: Dr. S.J Chopra, Dr. I.D Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta, Dr. P. Vijay and other faculty members of the Chemical Engineering and Petroleum and Earth Sciences Department and students.

Guest Lecture 2 - Mr Nitish Kumar Luthra

Guest of honor: Mr. Nitish Kumar Luthra, General Manager, Haldor Topsoe India Pvt. Ltd. 

On the 17th of October, the Department of Chemical Engineering and the students under it were enlightened about the demand for advancements in the field of hydroprocessing and catlysts. This was carefully taught by Mr. Nitish Kumar Luthra, General Manager at Process Refinery, Development and Engineering Production at Haldor Topsoe. The event began with by following the lighting of the ceremonial lamp and the Sararwati Vandana. The Chancellor Dr. S.J. Chopra and the Head of the Department Dr. I.D. Mall personally welcomed Mr. N.K. Luthra to the university.

He was clearly impressed by the welcome given to him and appreciated the university and its method of bringing forth budding engineers. Without further ado, he delved into his topic which was “Hydroprocessing: Its needs and Challenges”. For nearly an hour and a half, he captivated the students as they made to understand working of the hydroprocessing profession and the importance of improving the hydroprocessing techniques currently employed. He also explained the history of Haldor Topsoe and how the company manages to satisfy the various orders down to the slightest details for its large no. of customers. He also explained various other topics which included the importance of hydrogen in hydroprocessing, the difference between hydroprocessing and hydro-desulfurization and the various factors that affect the reaction kinetics of the reactions taking place in a reactor. It was quite the interactive session as many relevant questions were raised by students as well as well as from the faculty.

The event winded up with a summary to recall and jot down the important points discussed during the session. As a form of admiration and veneration, the Chancellor along with the rest of the dignitaries present gifted Mr. N.K. Luthra and his family a small token of respect in the form of a gift. Photographs were clicked and the event concluded with all the dignitaries present leaving for the lunch. The event managed to promote the motive of “teaching beyond the classrooms” and was added as another feather to the cap of the Chapter.


Attended By: Dr. S.J Chopra, Dr. I.D Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta, Dr. P. Vijay and other faculty members of the Chemical Engineering and Petroleum and Earth Sciences Department and students.

Guest Lecture 1 - Mr. Kartik Suryanarayan

Guest of honor: Mr. Kartik Suryanarayan Director (Engineering), UOP India Pvt. Ltd., Gurgaon

UPES witnessed the presence of an eminent personality from one of the world’s major technology providers, Honeywell UOP, Mr. Kartik Narayananan, Director (Engineering) at the headquarters of UOP LLC., in Gurgaon.

The event commenced with the Saraswati Vandana and the lighting of the ceremonial lamp by Mr. Kartik and some of the most celebrated faculty of the Chemical Department which included Dr. S.J. Chopra, Dr. I.D. Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta and Dr. P. Vijay. The occasion also had the highpoint of unveiling the first edition of IIChE UPES Student Chapter’s magazine “The Chemical Lifestyle”. Dr. S.J. Chopra welcomed Mr. Kartik to UPES and gave a great introduction about UOP LLC. to the spectators. Dr. I.D. Mall, the HOD of the Chemical Department then took the stage and introduced the guest of honor to the audience. Mr. Kartik took the stage and appreciated Dr. I.D. Mall for preparing a program where he could share his personal views on the crude oil prices, technology drivers and the skill set gap in the Indian workforce. He also appreciated the university about how it develops the students for an industry-centric career. The lecture covered a general area of how the oil market stands and the reasons for its fluctuations. It lasted for an hour and a half as Mr. Kartik carefully explained the world crude situation and how India stands a good chance as an emerging giant under the current conditions. He also talked about how the world and more importantly, India needs more innovative and creative engineers to solve the world’s problems. He also claimed that as engineers, we need to find ways to meet the energy requirements and to meet the ever-growing demands of the public with the resources at hand growing lesser day by day. The attendees of the lecture conducted by Mr. Kartik Suryanarayanan walked away in the end with a clearer picture and a better understanding of the oil economics and the geopolitical reasons behind the fluctuations of the oil prices.

The event turned out to be a grand achievement for the IIChE UPES Student Chapter and the Department of Chemical Engineering for it accomplished the objective of teaching the students about the current world scenario and the role of engineering in the advancement of the world.


Attended By: Dr. S.J Chopra, Dr. I.D Mall, Dr. S.K. Gupta, Dr. P. Vijay and other faculty members of the Chemical Engineering and Petroleum and Earth Sciences Department and students.
